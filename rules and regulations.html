Rules and Regulations

  • Students are required to arrive at school ahead of time and be present at the assembly no later than 7:50 am.
  • During the assembly, students are expected to display orderliness and maintain absolute silence while announcements are being made. Any form of hooting or shouting is strictly prohibited.
  • Students must be prepared for inspections of their nails, hair, uniforms, shoes, and other aspects whenever it is deemed necessary.
  • They depart from the assembly grounds to their classrooms in a disciplined manner, behaving like responsible and well-behaved young individuals.
  • Students are expected to use the toilet facilities appropriately, and failure to comply will result in the appropriate disciplinary measures being taken.
  • While on the school premises, students are required to communicate exclusively in English Language. The use of Pidgin or vernacular languages is not permitted except during designated Yoruba language sessions.
  • Engaging in any form of fighting is considered a severe offense that warrants suspension from school.
  • Students must refrain from using abusive language in any circumstances.
  • Chewing gum is strictly prohibited for students throughout the entire school compound.
  • Students are required to avoid leaving any pieces of paper or litter in both the classroom and the school compound. They should dispose of waste properly in designated bins.
  • Students are not permitted to loiter around in the staff room or along the corridors. They should adhere to designated areas for students and avoid disrupting the staff's workspace.
  • Students are not allowed to leave the school premises during school hours without obtaining proper permissions from the school authorities.
  • Students are expected to uphold and demonstrate obedience and respect for order and discipline within the school. They must also obey and show respect to all members of the staff, prefects, and individuals in positions of authority.
  • The practice of fagging and any form of brutality towards fellow students is strictly prohibited. Students should treat each other with kindness and consideration, regarding one another as brothers and sisters within the school family.
  • Any illegal collection of money or seizure of property belonging to fellow students is strictly forbidden. Such actions are not tolerated within the school community.
  • Cheating in examinations and tests, as well as stealing, are strictly prohibited, as they can lead to severe consequences, including suspension or even expulsion from the school. These actions undermine the principles of academic integrity and trust within the school community.
  • Students are not allowed to wear black bands or any other items that identify them as members of a cult. All activities related to cults are strictly prohibited within the school premises. Such measures are in place to ensure the safety and well-being of all students and maintain a positive learning environment.
  • Students are prohibited from bringing a large amount of money to school. This measure is implemented to prevent any potential issues or unnecessary risks related to the handling of substantial sums of money within the school setting.
  • Students are not permitted to bring electronic gadgets such as walkmans, transistor radios, cassette recorders, video cassettes, and magazines to school. This rule aims to maintain a focused and conducive learning environment and prevent distractions during class time.
  • Students are strictly prohibited from bringing a cellphone into the classroom. If any student is found with a cellphone, it will be confiscated and immediately destroyed as per the school's policy. This rule is in place to ensure that students remain attentive and focused during class time without distractions from electronic devices.

Punishment

Prefects have the authority to administer mild punishments to students who arrive late or cause disturbances in the school. Additionally, they may be assigned the responsibility of supervising punishments given by class teachers, section coordinators, or the Vice Principal. These punishments can include tasks such as emptying the dust bin, picking up pieces of paper, dusting the classroom, cleaning toilets, filling wash hand bowls with water, and cutting grass around the school premises. The purpose of these responsibilities is to instill discipline and a sense of responsibility among the students.

Punishments for more serious offenses, such as fighting or repeatedly skipping classes and homework, can be given by the Principal or Vice-Principal. These punishments may involve tasks like grass cutting or caning. The Principal has the authority to delegate the power to administer caning to any of the teachers, but no prefect is allowed to cane students. The aim of such punishments is to deter inappropriate behavior and foster a responsible and respectful learning environment within the school.

Suspension or Expulusion

The following offences attract expulsion as punishment:

  • Cheating in any examination whether internal or external or any other examination malpratice offence.
  • The use of drugs and intoxicants while in school.
  • The possession of offensive weapons with a view to harming teachers or fellow students.
  • Belonging to any secrete cult. Assaulting the Principal, Vice Principal or any other Teacher.
  • Any immoral act committed by student.

The following offences attract suspension as punishment:

  • Fighting violently and causing bodily harm to students.
  • Stealing of school items or items belonging to fellow students.
  • Failure to report an examination malpratice.
  • Assaulting a staff who is doing his/her legitimate duty either school or out of school.
  • Any other serious case of misconduct on the part of the students.
